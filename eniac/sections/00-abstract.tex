The Variational Quantum Eigensolver (VQE) depends on choices of parameterized
circuit, optimizer, and depth. We investigate supervised learning as a
meta-recommender that ranks configurations before execution. The evaluation
uses 486 noiseless simulations covering three molecules, 27 geometries, and 18
configurations per geometry, with nested cross-validation grouped by geometry.
An MLP achieved the highest observed top-3 coverage, 85.2\%, versus 77.8\% for
training-only global and per-molecule baselines, while evaluating three of 18
candidates at inference. However, the paired bootstrap interval included zero,
and leave-one-molecule-out evaluation showed weak generalization. Thus, the
approach is promising within related molecular families but remains preliminary.
