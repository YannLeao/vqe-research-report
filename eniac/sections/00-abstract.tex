The Variational Quantum Eigensolver (VQE) depends on choices of parameterized
circuit, optimizer, and depth. We use supervised learning to rank configurations
before execution. Tests cover 486 noiseless simulations of three molecules, 27
geometries, and 18 configurations per geometry, using nested cross-validation
grouped by geometry. An MLP reached 85.2\% top-3 coverage, compared with 77.8\%
for two simple selection strategies, while testing three of the 18 candidates.
The difference was uncertain, and performance weakened when a whole molecule
was excluded from training. The method may help with related molecules, but the
small noiseless dataset does not support broader conclusions.
